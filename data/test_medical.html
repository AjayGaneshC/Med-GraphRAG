Aspirin (Acetylsalicylic Acid)

Overview

Aspirin is a medication used to reduce pain, fever, and inflammation. It is also used as an antiplatelet agent to prevent blood clots.

Common Uses

  • Treatment of headaches and migraines
  • Cardiovascular disease prevention
  • Arthritis pain relief
  • Reduction of fever

Mechanism of Action

Aspirin inhibits the enzyme cyclooxygenase (COX), which is involved in the production of prostaglandins. This inhibition reduces inflammation and pain.

Drug Interactions

Aspirin may interact with:

  • Warfarin (increased bleeding risk)
  • Ibuprofen (reduced cardioprotective effects)
  • Corticosteroids (increased GI bleeding risk)

Side Effects

Common side effects include gastrointestinal upset, heartburn, and in rare cases, allergic reactions.
